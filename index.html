I'm a behavioral neuroscientist. I did my PhD in Behavioral Neuroscience at the University at Albany (SUNY), where my dissertation asked whether standard lab housing quietly inflates the cognitive effects we see in preclinical studies -- a question with real stakes for deciding which drug candidates are worth taking to the clinic.

Over the last 15 years I've worked at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, CUNY, and SUNY Albany. I'm a contributing author on a Cell paper and first author of a publication in CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics. I'm about equally at home at the bench and in R or Python -- wet-lab work on one side, meta-analysis and Bayesian modeling on the other. More about me
